Storj Support Policy

Last Updated: June 30, 2018.

This Storj Labs, Inc. (“Storj” or the “Company”) support policy (the “Support Policy”) encompasses all support obligations that Company has toward the contracted customers (“you” or “Customer(s)”) of Company products (“Products”). The Support Services shall begin as of the date specified in any ordering documentation by and between Company and Customer or, if no such date is specified, the date upon which Customer begins using Company’s Products. This Support Policy may be updated by the Company from time to time with or without notice. Please refer to this page periodically for any updates.

1. Definitions

For purposes of this Support Policy, the following terms have the meanings set forth below. Any terms not otherwise defined in this Support Policy shall have the respective meanings given to them in the Storj Terms of Service or in a separately executed Storj Customer Agreement between Company and Customer (each, a “Customer Agreement”).

(a) “Business Day” means a working day, Monday through Friday, excluding weekends and any U.S. bank holidays.

(b) “Bridge” means an application operated by Company, by Customer, or by a third party that allows Customer Applications to register accounts, access application credentials, facilitate storage and retrieval of Storage Materials on Storage Nodes, manage shard replication, and manage Storage Node reputation score.

(c) “Company Bridge” means a Bridge developed, operated, and hosted directly by Company.

(d) “Customer Application” means any web, mobile, or other application developed and/or used by Customer in connection with the Service.

(e) “Customer Cause” means any Error(s) caused by Customer, its Representatives or its End Users including without limitation any of the following:

i. Any negligent or improper use (including improper installation, configuration or implementation), misapplication, misuse or abuse of, or damage to, the Services, Storage Platform, LibStorj Library, or Storage Nodes by Customer or any of its Representatives;

ii. Any maintenance, update, improvement or other modification to or alteration of Services, Storage Platform, Storage Nodes or the Open Source Software by Customer or its Representatives that was not specifically authorized in writing by Company;

iii. Any use of the Services, Storage Platform, or Storage Nodes by Customer or its Representatives in a manner inconsistent with the then-current Documentation;

iv. Any use by Customer or their Representatives of any Third-party Products that Company has not provided to Customer;

v. Any use by Customer or their Representatives of a version of the Services, Storage Platform, or Storage Nodes that is earlier than the current major versions;

vi. Any Error caused by Customer Systems; or

vii. Any Error related to Out-of-Scope Services not covered by this Support Policy.

(f) “Custom Development” means custom software or products designed specifically to interact with the Services, Bridge, Storage Platform, or Storage Nodes developed:

i. by or on behalf of Customer, with or without Company’s assistance, using application programming interfaces (APIs) or other development tools related to the Products; or

ii. by Company for the benefit of Customer

(g) “Customer End Users” means the end users of Custom Development, Customer Systems, Third-party Products, or Customer Applications and any other customers or end users of Customer.

(h) “Customer Systems” means Customer’s information technology infrastructure, including Customer’s computers, software, databases, electronic systems (including database management systems) and networks.

(i) “Error” means with respect to the Service or any Company Bridge, any material, reproducible failure of the Services to operate in in substantial accordance with the then-current Documentation.

(j) “Incident” means the communication by Customer to Tier 1 Support of an Error or suspected Error.

(k) “LibStorj Library” means the asynchronous multi-platform client library and CLI for encrypted file transfer on the Storj network for use by Customer to create Customer Applications.

(l) “Open Source Software” means any open source software made available by Company pursuant to the terms of the open source license associated therewith.

(m) “Out-of-Scope Services” means any of the following:

i. Unless the ordering documentation specifically states that such services are covered under this Support Policy, any services requested by Customer for Custom Development, Customer Systems, or Third-party Products;

ii. Any services requested by Customer in connection with any apparent Error that Company determines in its reasonable discretion to have been caused by a Customer Cause;

iii. Any other services that Customer and Company may from time to time agree in writing are not included in the Support Services; and

iv. Any loss of an encryption key or encryption key seed.

(n) “Representative” means any employee or contractor of Customer, or any employee or contractor of a partner or Affiliate of Customer

(o) “Service” means the Company technology by which Customer Data is stored and retrieved from the Storage Platform comprised of the functions for encrypting, encoding, uploading and downloading Customer Data, and for creating and deleting user accounts. The definition of “Services” expressly excludes the Open Source Software, the Storage Platform, and the Storage Nodes.

(p) “Severity Levels” means the defined Error severity levels and corresponding responses, target Response Times, resolutions, and target Resolution Times referred to in the Severity Level Table set forth in Section 2(b) below.

(q) “Severity Level Table” means the table set forth in Section 2 below.

(r) “Support Hours” means 24x7x365 for Support Requests for Severity Level 1 Errors;and Business Days between the hours of 9:00 A.M. and 8:00 P.M. Eastern Time for non-Severity Level 1 Errors.

(s) “Storage Materials” means data and other materials stored by Customer on the Storage Platform.

(t) “Storage Node” means a third-party device that operates Open Source Software and is part of the distributed network comprising the Storage Platform.

(u) “Storage Platform” means a distributed network of Storage Nodes and one or more Bridges operated by Company or third parties that independently operate Open Source Software to facilitate storage of the Storage Materials.

(v) “Support Period” means the term during which Company provides the Support Services to Customer as set forth in any ordering documentation between Company and Customer or as specified in a Customer Agreement.

(w) “Support Services” means Company’s support of the Services and Company Bridge(s) as expressly stated herein but excluding any Out-of-Scope Services.

(x) “Third-party Products” means all third-party software, computer hardware, network hardware, electrical, telephone, wiring and all related accessories, components, parts and devices.


2. Support Responsibilities

(a) The Severity Level Table applies only to Errors properly reported by Customer in a Support Request as set forth herein;

(b) Response Times will be measured from the time Company receives a Support Request or, if the Support Request is received outside of Support Hours, then the next Support Hour following delivery of the Support Request until Company has responded to that Support Request;

(c) Resolution Times will be measured from the time Company receives a Support Request until Company has Resolved that Support Request;

(d) “Resolve”, “Resolved”, “Resolution” and correlative capitalized terms mean, with respect to any particular Support Request, that Company has corrected the Error, whether by a work-around or any other reasonable means, that prompted that Support Request;

(e) Company’s ability to correct Errors and to meet target Response Times and Resolutions Times is dependent upon and subject to, among other things, Customer timely meeting the Customer Obligations set forth below. Company is not responsible for Error correction where Customer has not timely and satisfactorily met the Customer Obligations.

3. Support Tiers
Support responsibilities are divided between Company and Customer into three (3) tiers of ownership:

For purposes of this Support Policy, the following terms have the meanings set forth below. Any terms not otherwise defined in this Support Policy shall have the respective meanings given to them in the Storj Terms of Service or in a separately executed Storj Customer Agreement between Company and Customer (each, a “Customer Agreement”).

(a) “Tier 1 Support” is provided by Company to Customer and Customer is responsible for providing Tier 1 Support to Customer End Users. Company Tier 1 Support is responsible for handling the initial communication with the Customer and for documenting the Incident in the form of a ticket. Tier 1 Support performs basic triage resulting in Resolution of the Incident or escalation to Tier 2. All communication with the Customer is provided by Tier 1 Support, which allows Customer to control the message to their end users. In order to communicate effectively with Customer about the status of their ticket, it is expected that Tier 1 Support will continue to communicate with Tier 2 Support on the progress of the ticket until the ticket is Resolved.

(b) “Tier 2 Support” is provided by Company. Tier 2 Support is responsible for advanced troubleshooting and Resolution of Incidents that require deeper expertise on the Company Storage Platform functionality.

(c) “Tier 3 Support” is provided by Company. Tier 3 Support is responsible for all incidents and problems that cannot be resolved in a timely manner by Tier 2 Support. Tier 3 Support includes engineers from product development and networking, as well as other functional specialists, including architects and security specialists.


4. Support Requests

(a) Contact. Customer may contact the Company Service Desk using one of the following 3 methods only (each, a “Support Request”):

i. Storj Community Support viaRocketChat which is hosted at https://community.storj.io/home

ii. Online at https://docs.storj.io/support.html

iii. For Severity 1 Errors Company provides a support email address at support@storj.io and as part of the operational support process.

(b) Customer Obligations
In order for a Support Request to qualify as an Error for Company to address pursuant to the applicable Service Level, Customer must describe the issue in detail including the time it was first observed by Customer and the circumstances, and including information required to reproduce the issue, and otherwise meet the definition of “Error” listed in the Severity Level Table above. Following receipt of a Support Request, if Company determines it refers to an Error, Company shall classify the Error in accordance with the Severity Level Table.


5. Support Tiers
Customer shall timely provide Company with all information, material, access, and assistance as reasonably requested by Company in connection with review of an Error including but not limited to the following (for purposes hereof, “Customer Obligations”):

(a) Output and other supporting data, documents and information, which may be required to identify the Storage Materials as defined in the Customer Agreement; and

(b) Remote access to the Customer Systems subject to Company’s compliance with all of Customer’s security and encryption requirements notified to Company in writing; and

(c) Such other reasonable cooperation and assistance as Company may request.


6. Support Request Escalation Process

(a) The Company Service Desk will use commercially reasonable efforts to address Support Requests and answer questions to resolve Support Requests within the Resolution Time specified in the Severity Level Table.

(b) In cases where the Company Service Desk is unable in the first instance to resolve the Error , it will be escalated for the next level of Incident Resolution.

(c) During the process of Incident Resolution, the Customer Technical Contact will be notified when the problem is solved or when Company changes the forecasted date of Resolution via email.

(d) To the extent the reported Error (Incident?) relates to external factors including but not limited to public, third party, Customer, or User software, networks, computers infrastructure, products, or services(collectively, “External Issues”), Company will inform the requester and close the request log. The Company is not responsible for addressing External Issues, and the terms of this Support Policy do not apply.


7. Supported Releases
Notwithstanding anything herein to the contrary, Company provides Support Services only for

(a) the current Major Release of the Software,

(b) any other Major Release that made generally available and backwards compatible with the Services, Storage Platform, or Storage Nodes.

For purposes hereof, “Major Release” means a new release of the Software as indicated by a change to the number to the left of the decimal point in the version number, for example, Version 2.1 upgraded to Version 3.0.
