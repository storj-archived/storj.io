FAQ

When will I get paid? How are payments calculated?

Storj Share payments are made once a month. So if you start farming right now, you will not receive a payment until early next month. We can't run the payment script until the month ends, then it needs to be reviewed by the community, and then rewards are finally paid out. We will make make this process faster and more transparent in the interface in the future. Specifics on the math behind payment calculations can be found here.

How to hold STORJ? What is a valid address?

In order to hold your STORJ, you need to store them in a ERC20 compatible Ethereum address to which you hold the private key yourself. Several options are available: Parity, Mist, and MyEtherWallet are some of the most used options.

If you accidentally send STORJ to a non-compatible wallet, it should be possible to export your private key to a compatible wallet. However, this can be sometimes difficult so it is recommended to always use a compatible wallet. Additionally, you should always keep your STORJ in a wallet you control the keys to, and not an exchange.

How to create a wallet?

To receive your Storj earnings, you need a valid ERC20-compatible Ethereum address to which you hold the private key yourself. Some, but not all options include Parity, Mist, and MyEtherWallet.

In case your wallet does not directly support ERC20 tokens, you can add the token as a contract. The general steps are:

  1. Access the option to watch a new contract. (Do not create a new contract).
  2. Add the contract address, 
    0xb64ef51c888972c908cfacf59b47c1afbc0ab8ac
  3. Give the contract a name, such as STORJ. If your wallet supports ERC20 tokens, the number of decimal places is 8.
  4. To send tokens or check balances, use the included methods and queries in the contract.

Where do I find the address?

You will receive STORJ to your Ethereum address, which starts with "0x" and is followed by 20 hex characters. It might look like this: 

0xb64ef51c888972c908cfacf59b47c1afbc0ab8ac

Your address can be used to send and receive STORJ. Once you receive STORJ, depending on your wallet it may or may not appear next to your ETH balance. To check your balance, you can use a third party block explorer, such as Etherscan or Ethplorer
